TITLE: Disaster Response Standard Operating Procedures
DEPARTMENT: State Disaster Management Authority
CATEGORY: disaster_sop

GAS LEAK: Turn off the gas regulator immediately. Open all doors and windows
for ventilation. Do not operate any electrical switch, fan, or lighter, as a
spark can ignite leaked gas. Evacuate everyone from the house. Call the LPG
emergency helpline 1906 and inform neighbours. Do not re-enter until a
technician confirms it is safe.

FIRE: Alert everyone in the building and evacuate calmly using stairs, never
the lift. Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire. Call 101 (Fire)
or 112. If trapped, seal the door gap with wet cloth and signal from a window.
Do not use water on an electrical fire; use a dry powder extinguisher or
switch off the mains first if safely possible.

FLOOD: Move to higher ground immediately. Switch off the main electricity
supply if water is entering the house. Avoid walking or driving through
moving flood water. Keep a battery radio or phone for official alerts. Call
1070 (State Disaster Control Room) for rescue assistance.

EARTHQUAKE: Drop, cover, and hold under a sturdy table until shaking stops.
Stay away from windows and heavy furniture that can fall. After shaking
stops, evacuate the building via stairs and move to open ground away from
structures and power lines.

ROAD ACCIDENT: Call 108 (Ambulance) and 100 (Police) immediately. Do not move
an injured person unless there is an immediate danger like fire, as
incorrect movement can worsen spinal injuries. Mark the location with hazard
lights or a warning triangle if available.
