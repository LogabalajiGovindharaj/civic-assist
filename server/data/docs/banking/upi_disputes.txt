TITLE: UPI Failed and Pending Transaction Resolution
REGULATOR: NPCI / Reserve Bank of India
CATEGORY: atm_upi_disputes

If a UPI payment shows as debited but the recipient does not receive it,
the amount is auto-reversed to the sender's account within 1-3 working
days (Turn Around Time, or TAT, defined by NPCI). No manual claim is
usually required in a straightforward auto-reversal case.

If the amount is not reversed within the TAT, raise a complaint through
the UPI app's "Help" or "Raise Dispute" section, referencing the UTR
(transaction reference) number, and also contact the bank's customer care.

Wrong transfer to an unintended UPI ID: contact your bank immediately with
the UTR number and ask them to intimate the beneficiary's bank. There is
no automatic reversal for a correctly processed transfer to a genuine but
unintended recipient; recovery depends on the beneficiary's cooperation or
a formal complaint through the bank and, if needed, the National Cyber
Crime Reporting Portal.
