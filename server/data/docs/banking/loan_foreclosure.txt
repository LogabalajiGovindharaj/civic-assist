TITLE: Home Loan Foreclosure and Prepayment Charges
REGULATOR: Reserve Bank of India
CATEGORY: loans

RBI directs that banks and NBFCs cannot charge foreclosure or prepayment
penalties on floating-rate home loans taken by individual borrowers, whether
the loan is closed using own funds or refinanced by another lender.

For fixed-rate home loans, and for loans to non-individual borrowers
(such as a business entity), the lender may still levy a prepayment charge
as per the loan agreement — this should be disclosed upfront in the Key
Fact Statement (KFS).

Required documents for foreclosure: loan account statement request, No
Objection Certificate (NOC) request, and (for property loans) original
title documents are returned within 30 days of full repayment as per RBI
rules; delay beyond that entitles the borrower to compensation of Rs. 5,000
per day of delay.

If charged a foreclosure fee on a floating-rate individual home loan, raise
a written complaint with the branch, then escalate to the RBI Banking
Ombudsman if unresolved within 30 days.
