TITLE: RBI/SEBI Investor and Consumer Advisory - Common Fraud Patterns
REGULATOR: Reserve Bank of India / SEBI
CATEGORY: fraud_advisory

Warning signs of financial fraud include:
- Guaranteed fixed high returns (for example, "12% monthly" or "double your
  money in 6 months") — no legitimate regulated investment can guarantee
  such returns.
- Pressure or incentive to recruit other investors (a sign of a pyramid or
  Ponzi structure).
- Entities that are not registered with RBI, SEBI, or IRDAI.
- Any request to share OTP, PIN, CVV, or full card number over a phone
  call, SMS, or WhatsApp message.
- Unsolicited instant-loan app links sent via SMS, often charging hidden
  fees and using aggressive, harassing recovery tactics.
- Fake customer-care numbers found via search engine ads rather than the
  bank's official website.

Legitimate banks, RBI, and SEBI never call or message asking for OTP, PIN,
password, or to install a remote-access screen-sharing app.

If you suspect fraud, do not confirm or deny that a specific offer is
"safe" — instead check the entity's registration on the regulator's
official website, and report suspicious activity to 1930 (Cyber Crime
Helpline) or the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal immediately.
